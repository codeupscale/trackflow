## Elevator Pitches

### 30-Second Version
TrackFlow is a workforce management platform that combines time tracking, activity monitoring, and HR management in one product. Unlike standalone time trackers, TrackFlow includes leave management, attendance, shift scheduling, and payroll. Unlike standalone HR tools, it captures real-time productivity data. And unlike both, it works offline. Growing distributed teams use TrackFlow to replace 4-6 separate tools with a single platform.

### 60-Second Version
Every growing distributed company hits the same wall: time tracking is in one tool, leave management is in spreadsheets, attendance is in a third system, and payroll requires manually pulling from all of them.

TrackFlow fixes this by combining time tracking, activity monitoring, screenshots, leave management, employee records, attendance, shift scheduling, and payroll into one platform.

What makes TrackFlow different from other time trackers is that it is built local-first. Every timer event is stored on the device before it reaches the server. If the WiFi drops, the laptop crashes, or the VPN disconnects, no data is lost. When the connection returns, everything syncs automatically.

For HR teams, TrackFlow automates the workflows that currently require email chains and spreadsheets. Employees apply for leave through a self-service portal. Attendance generates automatically from time entries. Payroll calculates from attendance data. The reconciliation step disappears.

We offer a 14-day free trial with all features included.

### 2-Minute Version
Let me describe the problem first. If you run a distributed team today, you probably use something like Hubstaff or Time Doctor for time tracking, BambooHR or Keka for HR, and Excel for the things that fall through the cracks. Leave management, attendance reconciliation, and shift scheduling often live in spreadsheets because no single tool handles all of them.

This creates two problems. First, your managers and HR team spend hours every month moving data between systems. Time entries go into one system, attendance into another, and then someone has to reconcile them for payroll. Second, your employees resent the friction. They log into one portal to track time, another to check leave balance, another to view their payslip. Five logins for five basic tasks.

TrackFlow solves this by being one platform for everything. We have a desktop agent that tracks time, activity, and screenshots with the same accuracy as Hubstaff. We have a web dashboard with full HR capabilities: leave management, employee records, attendance, shift scheduling, and payroll. And we have the reporting layer that ties it all together.

Three things make TrackFlow technically different. First, our time tracker is local-first. Every event writes to a local database on the employee's machine before reaching the server. If the network drops, nothing is lost. We have seen competitors where a WiFi hiccup during a timer stop causes the entire session to vanish. That cannot happen with TrackFlow.

Second, our screenshots capture each monitor individually. Competitors typically composite multiple monitors into a single image, which makes text unreadable on smaller screens. We capture per-display, which means the screenshots are actually useful for verification.

Third, we include SAML2 enterprise SSO in every plan. Competitors like Hubstaff gate SSO behind their highest tier. For companies with security requirements, this is a meaningful cost difference.

On the HR side, TrackFlow automates the manual reconciliation cycle. Time entries generate attendance records. Leave approvals deduct from balances automatically. Payroll pulls from attendance and salary structures to generate payslips with line-item breakdowns. The month-end spreadsheet exercise goes away.

We charge per seat, all features included, with a 14-day free trial. Companies typically see ROI within the first month from the HR automation alone.
