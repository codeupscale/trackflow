# TrackFlow Product Video Storyboard

**Duration**: 75 seconds (10 scenes)
**Aspect Ratio**: 16:9 (1920x1080), with 1:1 social cut
**Style**: Clean product showcase with minimal motion graphics. Real UI, not mockups.
**Brand Colors**: Warm amber-orange (#F59E0B primary), white backgrounds, dark slate text (#1E293B)

---

## Scene 1: The Problem
**Timestamp**: 0:00 - 0:07

**Frame 1 (0:00 - 0:03)**
- **Visual**: Desktop browser with 6 tabs visible in the tab bar. Each tab has a recognizable tool favicon and label: "Time Tracker," "HR Portal," "Leave Sheet.xlsx," "Email - Leave Request," "Payroll.xlsx," "Calendar." The browser is zoomed out enough to show all tabs clearly.
- **Camera**: Static shot, slight vignette to draw focus to the tabs.
- **Mood**: Cluttered, overwhelming. Slightly desaturated colors to convey fatigue.

**Frame 2 (0:03 - 0:05)**
- **Visual**: The tabs begin closing one by one with a soft click sound. Screen dims progressively.
- **Animation**: Each tab closes with a subtle left-to-right sweep. Timing: 0.4s per tab.

**Frame 3 (0:05 - 0:07)**
- **Visual**: Screen is dark. Large white text fades in: "6 tools. 1 problem." Hold for 1.5 seconds.
- **Mood**: Contrast. Clean after chaos. The viewer should feel relief.
- **Music**: Begins here. Soft piano note or ambient pad. Low volume.

---

## Scene 2: Introduction
**Timestamp**: 0:07 - 0:15

**Frame 4 (0:07 - 0:10)**
- **Visual**: Dark screen transitions to white. TrackFlow logo animates in from center. The logo is the primary amber-orange. It settles with a subtle shadow drop.
- **Animation**: Logo scales from 0.8x to 1.0x with ease-out over 0.6s. No bounce.

**Frame 5 (0:10 - 0:15)**
- **Visual**: Logo slides up as the TrackFlow web dashboard fades in below it. The dashboard shows the admin team overview: a sidebar with navigation, and the main panel showing 8-10 team members with online status dots (green), today's hours, and activity percentages.
- **Camera**: Slight zoom-in from 95% to 100% over 5 seconds, creating a subtle pull-in effect.
- **Mood**: Professional, clean, alive. The dashboard should look populated and real.
- **Music**: Beat picks up slightly. Adds a light rhythm.

---

## Scene 3: Time Tracking
**Timestamp**: 0:15 - 0:25

**Frame 6 (0:15 - 0:18)**
- **Visual**: The desktop agent system tray icon. A click expands it into the mini tracker window. The project dropdown opens, showing 3-4 project names. The cursor selects one. The "Start" button is clicked. A timer begins counting: 00:00:01, 00:00:02.
- **Camera**: Tight crop on the tray area, then expand to show the mini window.
- **Note**: Use actual desktop agent UI. The window should look lightweight and unobtrusive.

**Frame 7 (0:18 - 0:22)**
- **Visual**: Motion graphic: a simplified animation showing three icons in a horizontal flow. Left: laptop icon with a pulse (data captured). Center: local database icon (cylinder) with data flowing in. Right: cloud icon. An arrow connects local DB to cloud. The WiFi symbol between DB and cloud turns from green to red. The local DB continues to pulse (data safe). Then WiFi turns green. Data arrow flows to cloud.
- **Animation**: Smooth, minimal. Icons are flat design in the brand color. Arrows are animated lines.
- **Mood**: Technical confidence without complexity. The viewer should understand the concept in 4 seconds.

**Frame 8 (0:22 - 0:25)**
- **Visual**: Text card: "Local-first. Never lose a second." in large type. Small subtext below: "Survives crashes, restarts, and WiFi outages."
- **Transition**: Dissolve from animation to text card over 0.5s.

---

## Scene 4: Activity Monitoring
**Timestamp**: 0:25 - 0:35

**Frame 9 (0:25 - 0:29)**
- **Visual**: Web dashboard showing an employee's daily timeline. The timeline is a horizontal bar divided into segments colored by activity level: dark green (high), light green (medium), light gray (idle/no tracking). Below the timeline, screenshot thumbnails appear at regular intervals. The cursor hovers over a screenshot, and it enlarges to show a code editor.
- **Camera**: Start with full timeline visible, then slow zoom to the hovered screenshot.
- **Mood**: Informative. The viewer should see that this is useful data, not surveillance.

**Frame 10 (0:29 - 0:35)**
- **Visual**: Cut to the organization settings panel. The "Screenshot" section is visible with toggles: "Enable Screenshots: ON," "Screenshot Blur: toggle," "URL Tracking: OFF." The admin clicks the "Screenshot Blur" toggle to ON. A before/after shows a screenshot going from clear to blurred.
- **Camera**: Static shot on the settings panel.
- **Text overlay**: "Visibility. On your terms." appears at bottom right.
- **Mood**: Control. The viewer should feel that the organization is in charge, not the tool.

---

## Scene 5: Multi-Monitor Screenshots
**Timestamp**: 0:35 - 0:40

**Frame 11 (0:35 - 0:40)**
- **Visual**: Split screen with a vertical divider. Left half labeled "Others" in gray: shows a composited dual-monitor screenshot where text on both screens is clearly too small to read. Right half labeled "TrackFlow" in amber-orange: shows two separate screenshots side by side, each at native resolution with readable code and text.
- **Animation**: The divider slides in from the center. Left appears first (0.5s), then right (0.5s). A subtle zoom on the TrackFlow side highlights the readable text.
- **Mood**: Direct comparison. No ambiguity. The viewer should immediately see the quality difference.

## Scene 6: Leave Management
**Timestamp**: 0:40 - 0:47

**Frame 12 (0:40 - 0:43)**
- **Visual**: Employee self-service portal. The leave balance section shows cards for each leave type: "Annual Leave: 12 days," "Sick Leave: 5 days," "Casual Leave: 3 days." The employee clicks "Apply for Leave." A modal opens: leave type dropdown, date picker, reason field. The employee fills in and clicks "Submit."
- **Camera**: Screen recording of the actual UI. Cursor movements are smooth and deliberate.

**Frame 13 (0:43 - 0:47)**
- **Visual**: Quick cut to the manager's view. A notification badge appears. The manager opens the leave request, sees the employee name, dates, leave type, current balance, and a team calendar snippet showing who else is off that week. The manager clicks "Approve." Animation: the balance card updates (12 becomes 10). The team calendar highlights the new leave dates.
- **Camera**: Same style screen recording. Fast but not frantic.
- **Text overlay**: "Leave management. Self-service." at bottom.

## Scene 7: Attendance and Shifts
**Timestamp**: 0:47 - 0:53

**Frame 14 (0:47 - 0:50)**
- **Visual**: Attendance dashboard. A table shows employees with today's date. Status badges: green "Present," red "Absent," yellow "Half Day," blue "On Leave." Summary cards at top: "Present: 23," "Absent: 2," "On Leave: 4."
- **Camera**: Slight pan down the table to show it is populated with real-looking data.

**Frame 15 (0:50 - 0:53)**
- **Visual**: Transition to the shift roster view. A 7-day grid with employee names on the left and days across the top. Color-coded shift blocks (morning shift in blue, afternoon in green, night in purple). An employee's shift block has a small swap icon. Click shows a swap request modal. Quick approval animation.
- **Camera**: Pan across the roster to show the breadth of data.
- **Text overlay**: "Attendance. Shifts. Automated."

## Scene 8: Payroll
**Timestamp**: 0:53 - 1:00

**Frame 16 (0:53 - 0:56)**
- **Visual**: Payroll dashboard. A payroll period card: "April 2026 - Draft." The HR user clicks "Run Payroll." A progress bar fills. Status changes to "Processing" then "Completed." The number of generated payslips appears: "47 payslips generated."
- **Camera**: Focused on the payroll period card through the workflow.

**Frame 17 (0:56 - 1:00)**
- **Visual**: An individual payslip opens. Clean layout showing: Employee Name, Period, Gross Earnings (base salary, HRA, transport allowance as line items), Deductions (tax, insurance as line items), Net Pay in bold. The view transitions to the employee self-service portal where the same payslip is visible under "My Payslips."
- **Camera**: Zoom in on the payslip detail, then smooth transition to the employee view.
- **Text overlay**: "Payroll. From time to payslip."

## Music Suggestions

**Style**: Modern corporate ambient. Think "tech product launch" but warmer and less synthetic.

**Reference tracks** (for mood, not for licensing):
- "Inspire" by Bensound -- clean, uplifting, not overpowering
- "Corporate Technology" style on Artlist -- motivational without being cheesy
- Avoid: EDM drops, dramatic orchestral swells, ukulele/whistling (overused)

**Structure**:
- 0:00-0:07: Sparse. Piano or ambient pad. Let the silence of the "problem" contrast with the chaos visual.
- 0:07-0:40: Light rhythm builds. Add subtle percussion. The product is being introduced.
- 0:40-1:00: Maintain energy. HR features are being shown quickly.
- 1:00-1:10: Peak. Fullest arrangement. This is the "why choose us" moment.
- 1:10-1:17: Resolve. Single chord or soft resolution. Clean ending.
